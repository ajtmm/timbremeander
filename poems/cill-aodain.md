# Cill Aodáin

*by [Antoine Ó Raifteirí](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoine_%C3%93_Raifteiri)*
*Translation by Frank O’Connor (with adaptation by Niamh Hamill)*

And now, Spring! The days grow longer\
After St. Bridget’s day, I’ll raise my sail\
I’ll head off, and nothing will stop me\
Till I find myself back in the County Mayo. 

I swear that my heart swells\
Like the wind and the mist rises,\
Thinking of Carra and Gallen beneath it, \
Scahaveela and all the wide plains of Mayo;

Cill Aodan’s a town that’s lush and verdant,\
Berries and fruits of all sorts\
And if I were amongst my own kin,\
Old age would vanish, I’d be young again.
